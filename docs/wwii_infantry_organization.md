# WWII Infantry Organization — Research Notes

## Universal Hierarchy

All major powers shared the same ladder, named differently:

| Level      | US       | Germany  | USSR        | UK       |
|------------|----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| ~10 men    | Squad    | Gruppe   | Otdelenie   | Section  |
| ~3 squads  | Platoon  | Zug      | Vzvod       | Platoon  |
| ~3 platoons| Company  | Kompanie | Rota        | Company  |
| ~3 companies| Battalion| Bataillon| Batalon    | Battalion|
| ~3 battalions| Regiment| Regiment| Polk       | Brigade  |
| ~3 regiments| Division| Division | Diviziya   | Division |

## Unit Sizes by Power

### US Army
- **Squad** — 12 men: 1 Sergeant + 11 riflemen, 1 BAR automatic rifle
- **Platoon** — ~41 men: 3 squads + small HQ + 60mm mortar team
- **Company** — ~193 men: 3 platoons + HQ + weapons platoon (MGs, mortars)
- **Battalion** — ~871 men: 3 rifle companies + HQ + heavy weapons company

### German Heer — squad was MG-centred, not rifle-centred
- **Gruppe** — 10 men: 1 MG34/42 crew (gunner + loader) as the nucleus, rest are riflemen/ammo carriers
- **Zug** — ~50 men: 4 Gruppen + HQ
- **Kompanie** — ~180 men: 3 Züge + HQ
- **Bataillon** — ~860 men: 3–4 Kompanien

### Soviet Red Army — authorized strength rarely met; actual units often ran at 50–60%
- **Otdelenie** — 11 men
- **Vzvod** — ~50 men: 3 Otdeleniya
- **Rota** — ~178 men: 3 Vzvody
- **Batalon** — ~778 men: 3 Roty
- Divisions were called **Strelkovaya Diviziya** (Rifle Division), not "infantry division"

### British Army — 4 companies per battalion (unique among major powers)
- **Section** — 10 men: built around a Bren LMG team
- **Platoon** — ~36 men: 3 sections + HQ
- **Company** — ~127 men: 3 platoons + HQ
- **Battalion** — ~845 men: 4 companies

---

## Key Doctrinal Differences

- **Germany**: squad tactics MG-centric — the rifle group exists to protect and maneuver the machine gun. Tactical flexibility delegated to NCOs ("Auftragstaktik").
- **US**: more firepower at company level; relied on material superiority and set-piece coordination.
- **USSR**: mass and depth over precision; rifle divisions raised and expended at enormous scale.
- **UK**: 4-company battalion gave more flexibility in defence; sections were Bren-centric like German squads.

## Game Unit Tier Recommendations

| Game tier  | Historical basis        | Cols × rows | Men |
|------------|-------------------------|-------------|-----|
| Section    | Squad / Gruppe          | 4×3         | 12  |
| Platoon    | Platoon / Zug           | 10×4        | 40  |
| Company    | Company / Kompanie      | 20×8        | 160 |

Current prototype: 20×5 = 100 men → closest to a weak Company or strong Platoon.

**Conclusion:** The **Platoon** is the tactical atom — small enough to micromanage, large enough to have a meaningful formation. **Company** is the right level for NLP commands ("move the 2nd company to the ridge").
